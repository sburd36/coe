Design code of ethics for technology

Considering the ethical frameworks of Utilitarianism, Libertarianism, Deontology and Virtue Ethics, this code of ethics addresses a designers responsibility related to freedom, welfare, and virture and how the designer should handle the tensions between these.

By Abby Huang and Stephanie Burd

Statement

I swear to fulfill to the best of my ability and judgement, this covenant:

I will dedicate my design process to learning continuously, designing intentionally, and respecting the individuality of the people that use and interact with my designs.

I acknowledge my influence as a designer on the world and how my solutions will not be absolute, but an persistent attempt to advocate for the excellence of humanity.

Respect Individuality

By considering and respecting the individuality of users, we can encourage the deontological ideal of collective and individual greatness. It is necessary to allow people the freedom to make their own decisions, rather than dictating what is right or wrong.

Taking into consideration the libertarianism point of view, designers need to value the users right to decide what is best for them. As a designer, it is important to ensure that people are not rule by the technology the you design.

Kant emphasizes the idea that people are ruled by their pleasures, and it is important not to exploit this as a designer.

Activity

Consider it all?

A design game to align your design solution with the code of ethics.

Problem: You are designing a news app for teenagers to practice critical thinking.

Value Interview

Ask potential users whether use of the technology has positive or negative effects on a value they feel is important.

Consequences

Create a list of as many potentially implicated consequences as possible in five minutes.

Overall impact

How does your technology maximize benefit over harm?

Feedback Interview

Have participants write a brief description of their experience, needs and wants while using your technology and what their preferences were.

Freedom of choice

Are you giving your users more than one alternative when using your technology?

Universalize

If you were to use this app for all {target-users} in the world, who wouldn't be able to use your technology?
